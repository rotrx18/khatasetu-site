1. Why Balance Tracking is Your Safety Net

For a small business, "Balance" isn't just a number; it is your working capital. When you sell on credit (Udhaar) without proper tracking, you risk business failure. In 2026, losing track of even 5% of balances can wipe out your monthly profit.

2. Establish a Clear Credit Policy

1

Tracking starts before the sale. Decide who gets credit and what the limit is. A good policy includes a maximum credit limit and a set repayment cycle (e.g., 15 days).

3. Choosing Your Methodology

Manual books are the leading cause of balance errors. Digital tools like KhataSetu act as a central database that never forgets a decimal point. It is the only scalable way to manage 50+ customers efficiently.

Expert Tip: Categorize your customers into "Regulars" and "One-time" credit seekers to prioritize your follow-ups.

4. Real-Time Transaction Recording

Record it now, or lose it later. If you wait until closing time to update balances, you will forget small details. Using a digital ledger allows you to record entries while the customer is at the counter, building professional trust.

5. Automating Reminders

4

The most awkward part of business is asking for money. Automation removes this friction. Use digital systems to send automated balance updates via WhatsApp or SMS.

Conclusion: From Paper to Profit

Tracking balances is about building a professional relationship based on transparency. When your records are clear, your business commands respect. Stop guessing and start tracking today.
