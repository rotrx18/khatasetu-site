The Foundation of Business Liquidity

In the competitive retail and service landscape of 2026, offering credit is often a necessity to retain customers. However, unmanaged credit is the leading cause of small business failure. Customer Credit Management is the process of granting credit, setting terms, and ensuring timely recovery.

Without a system like KhataSetu, shopkeepers often find themselves with "paper profits" but no actual cash in the bank to pay suppliers. This guide breaks down how to balance growth with financial safety.

The 3 Pillars of Effective Credit Control

1. Credit Vetting (The Pre-Check)

Before offering a large line of credit, look at the customer’s history. Do they pay small amounts on time? In a digital ledger, you can see their payment patterns over the last 6 months instantly. If they are consistently late, they shouldn't get more credit.

2. Dynamic Credit Limits

Never offer "unlimited" credit. Every customer should have a limit in your KhataSetu app. Once they hit that limit, the system should prevent new credit sales until a payment is made. This "hard stop" saves you from catastrophic losses.

3. Automated Nudging

Most customers don't refuse to pay; they simply forget. Automated WhatsApp reminders change the psychology of the relationship. It's not "you" asking for money; it's the "system" sending a status update.

Rule of Thumb: If a customer's balance exceeds 20% of your monthly net profit, they are no longer a customer; they are a financial risk.

How to Recover Overdue Payments (Step-by-Step)

  1. The 3-Day Soft Reminder: Send a friendly KhataSetu report via WhatsApp 3 days before the agreed payment date.
  2. The Due Date Alert: Send a formal "Payment Due" notice with a clear link to pay or instructions for cash collection.
  3. The Grace Period Call: If 5 days pass, a personal phone call is required. Use the digital ledger to reference specific dates and amounts to avoid confusion.
  4. The Credit Freeze: Halt all further sales to the customer until the balance is cleared.
